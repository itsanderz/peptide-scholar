Retatrutide

GLP-1 / GIP / Glucagon Triple Agonist41 amino acidsLY3437943
Evidence BPhase IIIRx Required

Overview

Retatrutide is a novel triple hormone receptor agonist targeting GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors for the treatment of obesity and metabolic dysfunction. In clinical trials, it has demonstrated exceptional weight loss efficacy — up to 24% body weight reduction at 48 weeks.

Mechanism of Action

Retatrutide activates three distinct receptor pathways: GLP-1 slows gastric emptying and reduces appetite; GIP enhances insulin secretion and adipocyte function; glucagon increases energy expenditure through hepatic glucose production and lipolysis.

Benefits

  • Up to 24.2% body weight reduction at 48 weeks (Phase II trial)
  • Triple-mechanism synergy — appetite, energy expenditure, and fat metabolism
  • Significant HbA1c reduction in T2DM patients
  • Improved lipid profile (LDL, HDL, triglycerides)
  • Once-weekly subcutaneous dosing

Side Effects

  • Gastrointestinal: nausea (most common), vomiting, diarrhea
  • Injection site reactions — mild and self-limiting
  • Increased heart rate (2-5 bpm average increase observed)
  • Potential for hypoglycemia when combined with insulin
  • Pancreatitis risk (class effect — monitor symptoms)

FAQ

When will retatrutide be FDA approved?
Retatrutide is currently in Phase III clinical trials. If successful, FDA approval could come as early as 2026-2027.
How does retatrutide compare to Mounjaro?
Retatrutide is a triple agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon), while Mounjaro/tirzepatide is a dual agonist (GLP-1 + GIP only).
Is retatrutide available now?
Retatrutide is not yet FDA approved and is only available through clinical trials.

Retatrutide: The Triple-Agonist Breakthrough in Obesity Treatment

How a single molecule targeting GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors is reshaping the metabolic disease landscape.

JD
Dr. James Davidson
Medical Reviewer · Endocrinology
Reviewed
Key Takeaways
  • Retatrutide achieved 24.2% mean body weight reduction at 48 weeks in Phase II
  • Triple-agonist mechanism (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon) is unique among obesity therapies
  • Phase III trials are underway with potential FDA approval as early as 2026-2027
  • MASLD data shows significant liver fat reduction — expanding potential indications
Educational content only. This article is for informational purposes and does not constitute medical advice.

The landscape of obesity pharmacotherapy is undergoing a paradigm shift. While GLP-1 receptor agonists like semaglutide have dominated headlines, a new class of multi-agonist peptides is emerging — one that promises efficacy levels previously achievable only through bariatric surgery.

Retatrutide (LY3437943), developed by Eli Lilly, represents the most advanced triple-agonist approach in clinical development. By simultaneously targeting the GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors, it harnesses complementary metabolic pathways for unprecedented weight loss.

The Triple-Agonist Rationale

The evolution from single to multi-agonist peptides is grounded in the physiology of energy homeostasis. Each receptor target contributes a distinct metabolic signal:

  • GLP-1 — Slows gastric emptying, increases satiety, enhances glucose-dependent insulin secretion
  • GIP — Potentiates insulin secretion, improves adipocyte insulin sensitivity
  • Glucagon — Increases energy expenditure, promotes lipolysis, enhances fatty acid oxidation
⚡
Clinical insight: The glucagon component distinguishes retatrutide from tirzepatide — it doesn't just reduce calorie intake, it actively burns more calories.

Phase II Trial Results

At 48 weeks, the 12 mg dose group achieved a mean body weight reduction of 24.2%. Semaglutide produces ~14.9% at 68 weeks, and tirzepatide achieves ~22.5% at 72 weeks. Retatrutide's 24.2% at 48 weeks suggests faster and greater magnitude of effect.

"The efficacy observed with retatrutide is the highest mean weight reduction reported to date for any pharmacologic intervention in a Phase II obesity trial." — Jastreboff et al., Nature Medicine, 2024

MASLD and Beyond

Retatrutide's effects extend beyond weight loss. Data published in The Lancet Gastroenterology (PMID: 40609566, 2025) showed significant reductions in liver fat content and improvements in fibrosis markers in MASLD patients — a condition with no approved pharmacotherapy.

The Bottom Line

Retatrutide represents a genuine step-change in obesity pharmacotherapy. Its triple-agonist mechanism achieves weight loss approaching surgical benchmarks with an acceptable safety profile, positioning it as a central therapy in the future of metabolic disease management.

FAQ

Which is more effective for weight loss?
Based on Phase II data, retatrutide shows higher weight loss (24.2% at 48 weeks vs 22.5% at 72 weeks for tirzepatide). However, no head-to-head Phase III trial has directly compared them.
Can I get retatrutide now?
Retatrutide is only available through clinical trials. Tirzepatide is widely available by prescription (Mounjaro/Zepbound).
Which has a better safety profile?
Tirzepatide has a longer safety database. Retatrutide's profile is still being characterized in Phase III. Both share GLP-1 class effects.
Are both made by the same company?
Yes, both are developed by Eli Lilly, positioning them to offer a continuum of incretin-based therapies.
