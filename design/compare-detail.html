Retatrutide VS Tirzepatide

Retatrutide vs Tirzepatide:
Head-to-Head Comparison

Both developed by Eli Lilly, these two agents represent distinct generations of incretin-based therapy. Tirzepatide is a dual GLP-1/GIP agonist (FDA-approved). Retatrutide adds glucagon agonism for a triple mechanism. Here's how they compare across efficacy, safety, and clinical profile.

FAQ

Which is more effective for weight loss?
Based on Phase II data, retatrutide shows higher weight loss (24.2% at 48 weeks vs 22.5% at 72 weeks for tirzepatide). However, no head-to-head Phase III trial has directly compared them. Retatrutide's glucagon component likely drives the additional efficacy through increased energy expenditure.
Can I get retatrutide now? Should I wait?
Retatrutide is only available through clinical trials. Tirzepatide is widely available by prescription (Mounjaro/Zepbound). If you need treatment now, tirzepatide is the clinically appropriate choice. Retatrutide may offer advantages when approved, but it is not yet accessible.
Which has a better safety profile?
Tirzepatide has a longer and larger safety database as an approved drug. Retatrutide's safety profile is still being characterized in Phase III. Both share GLP-1 class effects (GI events), but retatrutide additionally causes a mild heart rate increase due to glucagon activation, the long-term significance of which is under investigation.
Are they both made by the same company?
Yes, both retatrutide (LY3437943) and tirzepatide (LY3298176) are developed by Eli Lilly. This positions Lilly to potentially offer a continuum of incretin-based therapies — from single to dual to triple agonism — depending on patient needs and disease severity.
