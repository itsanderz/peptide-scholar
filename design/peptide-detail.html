Retatrutide

GLP-1 / GIP / Glucagon Triple Agonist 41 amino acids LY3437943
Evidence B Phase III Rx Required

Overview

Retatrutide is a novel triple hormone receptor agonist targeting GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors for the treatment of obesity and metabolic dysfunction. In clinical trials, it has demonstrated exceptional weight loss efficacy—up to 24% body weight reduction at 48 weeks—surpassing existing single and dual agonist therapies.

Mechanism of Action

Retatrutide activates three distinct receptor pathways simultaneously: GLP-1 receptor agonism slows gastric emptying and reduces appetite; GIP receptor agonism enhances insulin secretion and improves adipocyte function; glucagon receptor agonism increases energy expenditure through hepatic glucose production and lipolysis. This triple activation produces synergistic metabolic effects beyond any dual-agonist approach.

Benefits

  • Up to 24.2% body weight reduction at 48 weeks (Phase II trial)
  • Triple-mechanism synergy — targets appetite, energy expenditure, and fat metabolism
  • Significant HbA1c reduction in T2DM patients
  • Improved lipid profile (LDL, HDL, triglycerides)
  • Once-weekly subcutaneous dosing

Side Effects

  • Gastrointestinal: nausea (most common), vomiting, diarrhea
  • Injection site reactions — mild and self-limiting
  • Increased heart rate (2-5 bpm average increase observed)
  • Potential for hypoglycemia when combined with insulin
  • Pancreatitis risk (class effect — monitor symptoms)

Retatrutide FAQ

When will retatrutide be FDA approved?
Retatrutide is currently in Phase III clinical trials. If successful, FDA approval could come as early as 2026-2027. The Phase II data showed exceptional efficacy, which has accelerated the development timeline.
How does retatrutide compare to Mounjaro?
Retatrutide is a triple agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon), while Mounjaro/tirzepatide is a dual agonist (GLP-1 + GIP only). Retatrutide's added glucagon agonism increases energy expenditure, resulting in greater weight loss in head-to-trial comparisons.
Is retatrutide available now?
Retatrutide is not yet FDA approved and is only available through clinical trials. It should not be purchased from research chemical suppliers claiming to sell it.
